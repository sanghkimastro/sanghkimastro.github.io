Welcome to Sanghyun Kim's homepage! I am an observational astrophysicist and a postdoctoral researcher at the Korea Astronomy and Space Science Institute (KASI). My research focuses on understanding the extreme conditions surrounding supermassive black holes (SMBHs) through multi-wavelength and multi-messenger observations of relativistic jets in blazars. To this end, I utilize high-angular-resolution Very Long Baseline Interferometry (VLBI) alongside data from multi-wavelength observatories, including the Fermi Gamma-ray Space Telescope and the IceCube Neutrino Observatory.
