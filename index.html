Welcome to Sanghyun Kim's Homepage

I am Sanghyun Kim, an observational astrophysicist.

I am a postdoctoral researcher at the Korea Astronomy and Space Science Institute (KASI). I focus on understanding the extreme conditions surrounding supermassive black holes (SMBHs) through multi-wavelength and multi-messenger observations of relativistic jets in blazars. My research aims to uncover the astrophysics of high-energy emission processes within these jets. To this end, I utilize high angular resolution Very Long Baseline Interferometry (VLBI), as well as multi-wavelength observatories, including the Fermi Gamma-Ray Space Telescope and the IceCube Neutrino Observatory.
