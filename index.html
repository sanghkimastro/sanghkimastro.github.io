Welcome to Sanghyun Kim's Homepage

I am Sanghyun Kim, an observational astrophysicist.

I am a postdoctral researcher at the Korea Astronomy and Space Science Institute (KASI). I have focused on understanding the extreme conditions around supermassive black holes (SMBHs) through multi-wavelength and multi-messenger observations of relativistic jets in blazars. Specifically, I have studied the innermost regions of these jets utilizing Very Long Baseline Interferometry (VLBI) at high angular resolution. My research aims to figure out the astrophysics of high-energy emissions.
